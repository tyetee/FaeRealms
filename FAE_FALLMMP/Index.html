What Are Fae?

Faeries are mythical creatures created through belief and folklore. They are assoiciated with nature and believed to work alongside mortals.The fae folk have the power to create illusions and glamour that benefit themself. They do not age the way mortals do but they are weak against Iron. Each fae is associated with an element, Fire, Air, Water, or Earth.

The Two Courts

The Unseelie and the Seelie Court

The Seelie Court is ruled by the Summer and Spring Courts, fae who are generally mischievious and witty.
They are free to visit the mortal world and enjoy to play, and interact with others.
They are populated by the more elegant and disciplined fae like the elves, gnomes, and hobgoblins.
The Unseelie Court is ruled by the Autumn and Winter Courts, fae who are violent and erratic.
They enjoy harming others and are merciless towards mortals.
They tend to lure mortals into their world by trickery and harm or even take pleasure in killing them.
Some of the faes populated there are changelings, drows, and spriggans.

The Faun

Fauns are half goat half human Mythological creatures.
Above the waist is usually human and below are the hooves of a goat,
with a tail at the back and horns at the head.
They are usually a symbol of fertitly.
In ancient Roman theses creatures were also viewed as gods.
They were believed to aid men traveling by guiding them.

Brownies

Brownies are seen in the English and Scottish folklore, as creatures who inhabits houses and barns.
Allow not seen they usually are up at night cleaning, and doing any other housework while you sleep.
As payment they take cream or bread and milk left out.

Dryads

Dryads are playful wood nymphs who live in trees.
They give their wisdom to those who need it and allure travelers.

Salamaders

Salamanders are fire spirits, they create as well as destroy

Kelpie

Kelpies are spirits in the water that have the form of an horse.
They allure mortals into the waters with offers of a ride,
and then drag their victims into the sea to drown.

Merfolk

Merfolk, are fish people who reside under the sea
Commonly known as Merrows, or Mermaids they enjoy singing and sometimes lure fishermen to their death.
It is believed that when a mermaid rescues you after drowning they either return you to land or turn you into a water fae.

Selkie

Selkies are water fae, who are found in the sea and ocean.
They appear as seal at first but shed their skin to reveal women underneath.

Trolls

Trolls are creatures who are known to be big and ugly
They turn to stone in the daylight, and at night they party.
In folktales they take mortals at night and eat them.

Pixie

Pixies are mischevious tiny creatures that enjoy playing tricks
They often take the form as hedgehogs and adore music and dancing

Where Are They?

There are many types of Faeries, too many to count, and they all have a role in our world.
Although Faeries are mythical folktale they add an exciting twist to mundane phenomenoms in our world.
